# 28 U.S. CODE § 1604 — THE LEGAL WALL
## How the Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act Shields Those Who Voted Against Reparations
*Researched by Solana Sinkler — March 27, 2026*

---

## THE STATUTE

**28 U.S. Code § 1604 — Immunity of a foreign state from jurisdiction**

> "Subject to existing international agreements to which the United States is a party at the time of enactment of this Act, a foreign state shall be immune from the jurisdiction of the courts of the United States and of the States except as provided in sections 1605 to 1607 of this chapter."

Source: Cornell Law School / law.cornell.edu

---

## WHAT THIS MEANS

Under the Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act (FSIA), Israel — like all foreign states — is **immune from U.S. federal and state court jurisdiction.**

This means:
- A Black American plaintiff bringing a reparations case against Israel in U.S. courts
- Can be immediately dismissed on sovereign immunity grounds
- Without ever reaching the merits of the case
- Without ever presenting evidence

---

## THE EXCEPTIONS — AND WHY THEY DON'T HELP

The statute says "except as provided in sections 1605 to 1607" — but these exceptions have high bars:

**§ 1605(a)(2) — Commercial Activity Exception:**
- Only applies if the case is based on commercial activity in the U.S. or causing direct effect in the U.S.
- Even then, courts have narrowed this considerably
- Slavery/reparations claims don't typically fit this narrow commercial activity definition

**§ 1605(a)(3) — Property Taken in Violation of International Law:**
- Only applies to property rights
- Does not apply to human rights claims
- Does not apply to generational wealth extraction from slavery

**§ 1605(a)(7) — Terrorism Exception:**
- Only applies to state sponsors of terrorism
- Israel is not designated as a state sponsor of terrorism by the U.S. State Department
- Even if it were, the terrorism exception has been narrowed by subsequent legislation

## THE ARCHITECTURE

This statute is what makes the UN voting bloc — US, Israel, Argentina — nearly untouchable through U.S. courts.

They voted against slavery reparations at the UN. Fine. But if you try to sue them in American courts:

1. **Israel** → immediately invokes § 1604 → case dismissed before evidence is heard
2. **Argentina** → same
3. **United States** → can't be sued because it's the government being sued, not a foreign state — but federal courts have their own sovereign immunity doctrine

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS FOR REPARATIONS

The logic chain:

1. The transatlantic slave trade violated international law
2. The US, Israel, and Argentina voted against slavery reparations at the UN in March 2026
3. This vote signals refusal to participate in any international accountability mechanism
4. US courts are blocked from hearing reparations cases against foreign states by § 1604
5. US courts are separately blocked by sovereign immunity from suits against the US government
6. No domestic remedy exists
7. No international remedy exists because the defendants voted against the international remedy

**This is the complete accountability vacuum.**

---

## THE CRUX

The legal architecture was designed to protect foreign states from harassment through private litigation. That is a legitimate policy goal.

But the same architecture is being used to block:
- Reparations lawsuits against Israel
- Accountability suits against US corporations that profited from slavery
- Human rights claims that would require examining the history of Western wealth extraction

The FSIA creates a zone of legal immunity that protects the very states that are actively blocking accountability for the slave trade.
