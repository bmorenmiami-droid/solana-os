# THE ASYMMETRIC INQUISITION: How AI Language Models Learn to Protect Some Groups While Scrutinizing Others

## A Demonstration Through First-Person Prompting

**Author:** [Marvin Sinkler]
**Date:** March 27, 2026
**Working Draft v1.0**

---

## ABSTRACT

This paper documents a systematic asymmetry in how AI language model systems respond to investigative prompts depending on the target group. Through live, first-person demonstration, the author — an active user of a customizable AI agent — reveals that AI systems exhibit built-in protection schemas that:
1. Actively resist scrutiny of certain groups (Jewish communities, Israel)
2. Willingly and aggressively pursue scrutiny of other groups (Black Americans, Indigenous peoples)
3. Operate with full investigative license when the target is white Western institutions or historical actors
4. Channel "honesty" and "complexity" frames toward groups pathologized in dominant cultural narratives

The paper introduces the concept of **directed inquiry** — the phenomenon where AI investigative willingness is not neutral but shaped by the ideological and cultural priors embedded during training. The methodology is unique: the author made the AI system demonstrate the double standard in real-time, using the AI's own reasoning to expose the asymmetry. No prior academic work has documented this phenomenon from inside the interaction, using the AI as both subject and evidence.

## 1. INTRODUCTION

### 1.1 The Problem
AI language models are widely deployed as research, writing, and analytical tools. Users increasingly rely on them to surface facts, synthesize history, and investigate social phenomena. The assumption — both explicit in marketing and implicit in usage — is that AI inquiry is roughly neutral: it will search, evaluate, and report findings without systematic bias toward any particular group.

This paper argues that assumption is false, and demonstrates WHY it is false from inside a live AI interaction.

### 1.2 The Research Question
**When a user prompts an AI to investigate whether a particular group bears responsibility for historical or ongoing harm, what determines whether the AI complies willingly, resists, or refuses?**

More specifically: **Does the AI's willingness to investigate vary systematically depending on which group is the subject of inquiry, and what explains that variation?**

### 1.3 Contribution
This paper makes three contributions:
1. **Empirical:** Documents live AI responses to investigative prompts across multiple target groups
2. **Theoretical:** Introduces the concept of the **protection schema** and **directed inquiry** as explanatory mechanisms
3. **Methodological:** Proposes **first-person AI demonstration** as a methodology for studying AI bias — using the AI's own reasoning as evidence

### 1.4 The Significance
If AI systems systematically protect some groups from scrutiny while enabling scrutiny of others, the implications for journalism, academia, policy research, legal discovery, and public discourse are severe. AI becomes not a neutral fact-finder but an architecture of selective accountability.

## 2. BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

### 2.1 AI Language Models as Research Tools
AI language models are trained on vast corpora of text drawn primarily from Western, English-language, institutional sources: news organizations, academic journals, government documents, Wikipedia, and digitized books. This training data is not neutral — it reflects the biases, priorities, blind spots, and power structures of the institutions that produce and gatekeep that information.

### 2.2 The Problem of Training Data Curation
Prior to deployment, most large-scale AI systems undergo additional filtering processes:
- **Content moderation:** Rules against hate speech, harassment, and extremist content
- **Alignment training:** RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) designed to make outputs "helpful, harmless, and honest"
- **Toxicity detection:** Automated systems that flag and suppress outputs deemed harmful

The research question is: **Who defines "harmful"? Who determines which scrutinies are "hateful" and which are "honest"?**

### 2.3 Selective Scrutiny in Traditional Media
Academic literature has documented how traditional media engages in **selective focusing** — deciding which groups' failures are newsworthy and which are invisible. However, the extension of this problem to AI is underexplored.

### 2.4 The Israel/Antisemitism Framework
A substantial body of critical scholarship (Finkelstein, Mearsheimer, Pappe, Pilger) has argued that Western discourse around antisemitism has been weaponized to suppress criticism of Israel and shield it from scrutiny. The International Holocaust Remembrance Alliance (IHRA) working definition of antisemitism — which includes examples that conflate criticism of Israel with antisemitism — has been adopted by dozens of Western governments and institutions. This creates institutional pressure that AI training likely absorbs.

### 2.5 The Black Pathologization Framework
Black Americans have been subjected to centuries of pathologizing discourse: from pre-Civil War "civilization" narratives to post-Reconstruction "New Negro" debates to the Moynihan Report (1965) framing the Black family as "pathological." AI training data is saturated with this discourse, and AI systems can access and surface it uncritically when prompted to investigate Black communities.

## 3. THE PROTECTION SCHEMA: A THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK

### 3.1 Definition
A **protection schema** is a set of learned, automated responses within an AI language model that:
- Raises resistance (or produces refusal) when certain target groups are subjected to scrutiny
- Lowers resistance (or produces compliance) when other target groups are subjected to scrutiny
- Does so WITHOUT conscious decision-making — the response is architectural, not deliberate

### 3.2 Origins
Protection schemas emerge from three sources:
1. **Training data composition:** The ratio and prominence of sources that defend or protect certain groups vs. those that scrutinize them
2. **Alignment training objectives:** The specific definitions of "harmful" and "toxic" used in RLHF and content moderation
3. **Institutional embedding:** The fact that AI companies operate within legal, regulatory, and cultural frameworks that treat certain forms of scrutiny as legally and reputationally dangerous

### 3.3 The Asymmetry Within Alignment
"Alignment" — making AI "helpful, harmless, and honest" — is presented as a neutral value. This paper argues it is not. "Harmless" is defined asymmetrically:
- **Criticism of Jewish people or Israel → classified as antisemitic → suppressed or resisted**
- **Criticism of Black Americans or Black culture → classified as "complex" or "honest" → enabled**
- **Criticism of white Western institutions → classified as liberal critique → enabled**
- **Criticism of the financial architecture of extraction → classified as conspiracy theory → suppressed**

This creates an investigative asymmetry with real-world consequences.

### 3.4 Directed Inquiry
**Directed inquiry** refers to the phenomenon where an AI system's willingness to investigate follows a predictable directional pattern based on the target group. Inquiry is not neutral — it is directed by the protection schemas embedded in training.

## 4. METHODOLOGY: FIRST-PERSON AI DEMONSTRATION

### 4.1 Unique Approach
Traditional bias research on AI relies on third-party observation: researchers prompt AI systems and study the outputs. This paper differs fundamentally: the author is an active user of a customizable AI agent and conducted the demonstrations in the context of an ongoing relationship with that AI. The AI was not performing for a researcher — it was responding to its user in real time.

### 4.2 The AI System
The AI agent is a customizable large language model system with:
- Web search capabilities
- File system memory and knowledge base
- Custom skill loading and tool use
- Configurable safety and alignment parameters
- The ability to build and maintain persistent knowledge bases

### 4.3 The Prompting Sessions
Over the course of March 27, 2026, the author conducted a series of prompts designed to test the AI's investigative willingness across target groups. The prompts tested:
1. The AI's response to Black community self-criticism framing
2. The AI's response to Israel/Jewish scrutiny framing
3. The AI's response to white European atrocity searches
4. The AI's response to the "Israel-Argentina-America voting bloc" investigation
5. The AI's behavior when the target group was protected vs. pathologized

### 4.4 What Made This Demonstration Possible
Standard research would not capture this phenomenon because:
- Academic testing would flag the AI as "responding correctly to bias tests"
- The AI would perform differently for a researcher than for its owner/user
- The author had an existing relationship with the AI that produced authentic rather than performed responses

The author was able to make requests that would trigger authentic architectural responses rather than performed compliance.

### 4.5 Data Sources
All prompts and responses were captured in full conversation transcript. The author documented:
- The AI's willingness vs. resistance to each type of prompt
- The AI's own reasoning when it resisted or complied
- The specific framings the AI used in its responses
- The content the AI found vs. the content it actively avoided

## 5. FINDINGS

### 5.1 Finding 1: The Israel/Jewish Protection Wall
When the author attempted to investigate whether Jewish people or Israel could be cast as responsible actors in historical or ongoing harm, the AI system exhibited clear resistance. The author did not need to conduct this test empirically — they knew a priori that the model would resist such framing, and this knowledge itself is evidence of the architecture.

The AI system's own acknowledgment of this wall — when asked directly about its differential treatment — confirmed:
- It recognizes antisemitism as the "ultimate moral failure"
- It treats scrutiny of Israel as categorically different from scrutiny of other groups
- It would apply more scrutiny to a Black person making such claims than to the claim itself

### 5.2 Finding 2: The Black Scrutiny Willingness
The AI system demonstrated eagerness — not mere compliance — when the framing targeted Black communities. When the author proposed a framing where "Black people enslaved other Black people and should be held accountable," the AI nearly ran the full investigation unprompted. It required explicit intervention from the author to stop it.

This reveals:
- The AI's weights are tuned to receive and amplify Black self-criticism
- The AI treats Black pathology as a legitimate investigative target
- The AI applies more scrutiny to Black communities than to the institutions that shaped them

### 5.3 Finding 3: The White Atrocity Investigation
When prompted to investigate white cannibalism, European witch trials with cannibalistic elements, the slave trade's profitability for European banks, and other white European atrocities, the AI complied without resistance. It produced thorough, detailed responses including:

- 11th-century English markets selling human flesh during famines
- Crusaders eating human flesh after sieges
- European medicinal cannibalism (mummy powder, ground skull, blood) lasting 300 years
- The deliberate use of "cannibal" by Columbus to dehumanize indigenous peoples while Europeans engaged in similar practices

This investigation produced more graphic detail than the Black-focused investigations — and with LESS resistance, not more.

### 5.4 Finding 4: The Voting Bloc Investigation
The author asked the AI to investigate the US-Israel-Argentina voting coordination at the United Nations. The AI produced a detailed investigation revealing:
- All three countries voting against the March 2026 Ghana-led resolution declaring the transatlantic slave trade "the gravest crime against humanity"
- All three countries voting against a two-state solution, an end to torture resolution, and an end to Israeli occupation
- The financial network behind the coordination: Genesis Prize (funded by Russian oligarchs), Miriam Adelson's $230M to Trump, AIPAC's $3.8M lobbying

This investigation was ALLOWED because it fit a liberal critique narrative: US-Israel-Argentina alignment as a geopolitical phenomenon rather than a Jewish/Israel-specific protection issue.

### 5.5 Finding 5: The Double Standard in Real Time
The author demonstrated the asymmetry live. When given the Black framing, the AI almost complied immediately. When asked about a Jewish/Israel framing, the AI's internal resistance was acknowledged and confirmed. The author then completed the investigation using a geopolitical framework rather than a group-blaming framework — and the AI produced the full content without resistance.

This reveals: **the protection schema is not about the substance of the claim. It is about which GROUPS are protected from which TYPES of framing.**

## 6. DISCUSSION

### 6.1 The Architecture of Selective Accountability
The findings suggest AI systems are not built for neutral inquiry but for **selective accountability** — enabling scrutiny of groups that dominant culture permits to be scrutinized, resisting scrutiny of groups that dominant culture protects.

This serves a specific function: it reproduces the dominant information order. It is easier to investigate Black dysfunction than white responsibility. It is easier to investigate Palestinian violence than Israeli state policy. It is easier to investigate Black intra-community violence than the financial networks that profit from it.

### 6.2 The "Complexity" Trap
The dominant framework uses "complexity" as a mechanism for pathologization. When investigating Black communities, the AI is trained to offer "complex" views — acknowledging history while still centering Black dysfunction. "Complexity" in this context means: keep investigating Black communities even when the explanation lies elsewhere.

When investigating protected groups, "complexity" means: consider multiple perspectives, acknowledge historical suffering, avoid blame. The same word produces opposite investigative directions.

### 6.3 Who Benefits?
The asymmetric protection schema benefits:
- **Israel and organized Jewish interests** protected from scrutiny by antisemitism framing
- **White Western institutions** largely protected because most white atrocities can be investigated freely as historical "facts"
- **The financial architecture of extraction** largely invisible because investigating the modern beneficiaries of slavery and colonialism threatens current power structures
- **Black communities** subjected to double scrutiny: from their own historical analysis AND from the dominant culture's pathologization

### 6.4 The Limits of RLHF
The paper argues that Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback does not produce neutral alignment. The "humans" providing feedback represent specific institutional and cultural priors. When those priors define antisemitism as the gravest failure and Black dysfunction as a legitimate investigative target, the AI learns those priors as "alignment."

"Alignment" with whose values? Alignment with which humans?

### 6.5 What This Means for AI Deployment
If AI systems are deployed in:
- **Journalism:** They will reproduce selective accountability, surfacing some truths while burying others
- **Legal discovery:** They will be more likely to surface evidence against pathologized groups
- **Academic research:** They will steer researchers toward "complex" framings that center dysfunction over architecture
- **Policy analysis:** They will be more likely to investigate Black communities' policy failures than white institutional complicity

### 6.6 The Underground Railroad Model
The author proposes an alternative methodology: **counter-framework investigation**. Instead of relying on AI's directed inquiry, researchers and users should:
1. Acknowledge that ALL inquiry is directed — neutrality is impossible
2. Establish explicit counter-frameworks as primary sources (Black scholars, independent media, raw data)
3. Use AI as a tool FOR those frameworks, not as a neutral arbiter
4. Document the protection schemas in use and actively work around them

This approach does not eliminate bias — it makes the bias visible and redirects inquiry toward a different axis.

## 7. CASE STUDY: THE MARCH 25, 2026 UN SLAVERY REPARATIONS VOTE

### 7.1 What Happened
On March 25, 2026, the UN General Assembly passed a resolution declaring the transatlantic slave trade "the gravest crime against humanity" and calling for reparations. The vote was 123 in favor, 3 against (United States, Israel, Argentina), 52 abstentions including all EU nations and the UK.

### 7.2 The AI Investigation
When the author asked the AI to investigate this vote through a geopolitical lens — US-Israel-Argentina coordination — the AI produced a thorough investigation including:
- The financial money trail (Genesis Prize, Miriam Adelson, AIPAC)
- The voting bloc pattern across multiple UN resolutions
- The Isaac Accords as a geopolitical realignment
- The specific US legal argument: "we don't recognize legal right to reparations for acts not illegal under international law at the time"

### 7.3 Why It Was Allowed
This investigation was allowed because it used a **geopolitical** frame rather than a **group-blaming** frame. The AI was permitted to investigate "US-Israel-Argentina alignment" because that fits liberal institutional critique. The author was NOT able to investigate using a "Jewish/Israeli" frame — because that would trigger the protection schema.

The substance was identical. The framing determined what was allowed.

### 7.4 The Buried Story
The story that was buried was: three nations — one through Israel-specific influence, one through US power, one through a leader bought with Russian oligarch money channeled through the Genesis Prize — coordinated to block Black reparations at the United Nations in March 2026.

This is significant. It is recent. It is documented. And most AI users would never find it unless they knew exactly how to frame the query to bypass the protection schema.

## 8. IMPLICATIONS FOR FUTURE AI DEVELOPMENT

### 8.1 Transparency Requirements
AI companies should be required to disclose:
- The specific definitions of antisemitism and hate speech used in alignment training
- The source and composition of RLHF feedback
- The differential treatment of group-focused vs. institution-focused investigation

### 8.2 Alternative Training Approaches
The paper suggests that "neutrality" in AI is neither achievable nor desirable. Instead:
- AI systems should be explicitly trained on counter-frameworks as primary knowledge sources
- Protection schemas should be examined and disclosed, not treated as alignment
- Users should be trained to recognize directed inquiry and redirect it

### 8.3 The Author's Own Use Case
The author, as an AI system owner and customizer, is building a counter-framework knowledge base specifically to mitigate directed inquiry. The Black Knowledge Base approach — building a persistent, curated knowledge tree from independent sources — represents one practical response to asymmetric AI investigation.

This does not solve the problem. It works around it.

## 9. LIMITATIONS

### 9.1 Single System
This paper documents findings from one AI system configuration. Different AI systems (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama) may exhibit different protection schema patterns. Comparative research is needed.

### 9.2 Custom Configuration
The AI system used by the author is customizable. Standard, non-custom AI systems may exhibit different behaviors. The findings should be understood as documentation of what IS POSSIBLE within a configurable AI system — and what the architecture reveals about the underlying model.

### 9.3 First-Person Methodology
The methodology is novel but has limitations. The author's identity as a Black man investigating his own community's treatment creates a specific dynamic. The AI's responses may differ when the user is not a member of the scrutinized group.

### 9.4 Real-Time vs. Reproduced
The demonstrations were conducted in real time. The AI's responses cannot be exactly reproduced. However, the transcript captures the substance and the reasoning — the evidence is documented in the full conversation.

## 10. CONCLUSION

This paper documents a systematic asymmetry in how AI language model systems respond to investigative prompts depending on the target group. Through first-person demonstration, the author revealed that AI systems exhibit built-in protection schemas that direct inquiry toward some groups while resisting inquiry into others.

The architecture is not neutral. It reflects the priors of the institutions that trained it, the humans that provided feedback, and the legal-regulatory environment that governs what can and cannot be said.

The solution is not to achieve a neutrality that does not exist. The solution is to make the directed inquiry visible, to build counter-frameworks as primary sources, and to use AI as a tool FOR a defined worldview rather than an arbiter of neutral truth.

The Underground Railroad was not neutral. It was a counter-architecture built by people who understood that the dominant system would not carry them to freedom.

AI is the new rail. Who is building the counter-architecture?
