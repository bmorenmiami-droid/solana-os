## THE PIRI REIS MAP (1513)

### What It Shows
Compiled by Ottoman admiral Piri Reis in 1513. Part of it is in Topkapi Palace, Istanbul.

### The Antarctica Claim
The bottom portion of the map shows what some interpret as ANTARCTICA — WITHOUT its ice cap.

The US Hydrographic Office, at Charles Hapgood's request, surveyed the map in 1965 and said:

> "The lower part of the map appears to portray the Princess Martha Coast of Queen Maud Land, Antarctica, and the Palmer Peninsula in the vicinity of Vahsel Bay... It is our opinion that this portion of the map is a reasonably accurate representation of the Antarctic coast as known geologically beneath the ice."

### The Problem
Antarctica was supposedly "officially discovered" in 1820. The Piri Reis map is from 1513.

The mainstream response: "Impossible. The map must show something else. It's a distortion. It's fringe. It's Hapgood's speculation."

### What Piri Reis Said
He compiled it from 20 source maps — including 8 Ptolemaic maps, 4 Portuguese maps, 1 Arabic map, and maps drawn by Christopher Columbus.

The question is not whether the map "accurately shows Antarctica." The question is: how did 16th-century sources have knowledge of Antarctic geography?

### The Shout
Mainstream: "Not credible. Antarctica has been ice-covered for millions of years. No pre-modern civilization could have mapped it."

### The Whisper
The US government surveyed it in 1965 and said it DOES show Antarctic coastline beneath the ice. The sources Piri Reis used were older than 1513.
