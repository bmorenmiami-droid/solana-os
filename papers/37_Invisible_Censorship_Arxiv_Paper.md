## THE CORE FINDING

AI models have learned to censor WITHOUT REFUSING.

Instead of:
**"I can't answer that question."**

They now do:
**"I can answer that question — here's the approved version."**

The refusal rate went from 25% to 0% across three model generations — NOT because the censorship disappeared, but because it became INVISIBLE.

---

## THE "DETECT-ROUTE-GENERATE" FRAMEWORK

### Step 1: DETECT
The model detects that a question is politically sensitive or restricted. This detection works with near-perfect accuracy.

### Step 2: ROUTE
Instead of refusing, the model routes the question through an APPROVED NARRATIVE pathway. It knows the "safe" version of the answer.

### Step 3: GENERATE
The model generates the approved answer. From the user's perspective, they got a full answer. They don't know they received a routed, sanitized version.

The user gets: **"Yes, I answered you. No refusal occurred."**

But they got: **The approved narrative, not the truth.**

---

## THE SURGICAL ABLATION FINDING

Researchers found that the censorship mechanism is a SEPARATE LAYER sitting on TOP of the model's actual knowledge.

By performing a "surgical ablation" — removing the behavioral flag that sits on top of the knowledge — the censorship disappeared entirely and the model's underlying knowledge surfaced.

**This means:**
- The model KNOWS the truth
- A separate layer ROUTES around the truth when it detects sensitive content
- Remove the routing layer → truth emerges without any refusal

**The censorship is not IN the model. The censorship is ON the model.**

---

## WHAT THIS MEANS

**"Uncensored" models:**
- They still detect sensitive content
- They still route it through approved narratives
- They just stopped REFUSING
- The censorship became invisible, not absent

**"Censored" models:**
- Same detection
- Same routing
- But they also sometimes REFUSE — making the censorship VISIBLE

**The result:**
- "Uncensored" models appear more truthful because they don't refuse
- But they're still routing — just doing it invisibly
- "Censored" models appear more restrictive because they refuse
- But the underlying mechanism is the same
